# MAGI Terms of Service

**Status:** Commercial template for operator review.

These terms are a starting point for a hosted, managed, or paid MAGI service.
They do not replace the open-source license for the code, and they are not legal
advice. Before charging customers, the service operator should fill in the
legal entity, contact information, governing law, pricing terms, and any local
consumer or professional-service requirements.

## 1. Service

MAGI provides local-first AI workflow automation, document processing, routing,
summarization, translation, scheduling, and operator-configured integrations.
Specific modules may include legal workflow automation, but MAGI is a software
tool and does not independently provide legal advice.

## 2. Operator and Customer

The party deploying or offering MAGI to users is the "Operator." The person or
organization using the service is the "Customer." In self-hosted deployments,
the Customer may also be the Operator.

The Operator is responsible for:

- Configuration, credentials, account ownership, backups, and access control.
- Human review of generated outputs.
- Compliance with professional, privacy, consumer-protection, and data-handling
  obligations that apply to the Customer's use case.

## 3. Acceptable Use

Customers must not use MAGI to:

- Access systems or data without authorization.
- Upload unlawful, infringing, or malicious content.
- Bypass third-party portal rules, rate limits, account controls, or terms.
- Submit legal, financial, medical, or government filings without qualified
  human review where required.
- Publish private case material, personal data, secrets, or credentials.

## 4. AI Output and Human Review

MAGI can make mistakes. Outputs may be incomplete, outdated, biased, or
incorrect. Customers must independently verify important results before relying
on them, especially for legal, financial, safety, employment, government, or
medical matters.

No output should be treated as professional advice unless a qualified
professional has reviewed and adopted it.

## 5. Third-Party Services

MAGI may integrate with messaging services, calendars, email, document
libraries, model providers, NAS/file storage, databases, and official or
operator-owned portals. Those services are governed by their own terms,
availability, rate limits, and privacy policies.

The Operator is responsible for confirming that each third-party dependency is
licensed and configured for commercial use.

## 6. Customer Data

Customer data is handled according to the applicable privacy policy and data
retention policy. Unless a separate agreement says otherwise:

- The Operator should process only data needed to provide the service.
- Customer data should not be used to train models without explicit permission.
- Secrets and credentials should never be submitted through public support
  channels.
- Deletion and export requests are handled according to the published support
  process and applicable law.

## 7. Security

Customers must protect their accounts, devices, credentials, webhook URLs,
tokens, and backup media. The Operator must provide a vulnerability reporting
channel and maintain production security controls described in `SECURITY.md`.

## 8. Fees and Paid Plans

agreement.

Unless a signed agreement states otherwise, MAGI is provided without an uptime
commitment. Support scope and response targets are described in `SUPPORT.md` or
in a separate commercial support agreement.
